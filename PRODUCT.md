# Product

## Register

product

## Users

TaxoFix BD is used by a primary Bangladesh tax and VAT service firm, its internal staff, client users, and approved partner firms. Firm owners and admins need oversight across clients, cases, documents, VAT periods, submissions, and partner capacity. Tax managers, accountants, document checkers, and support staff need clear daily work queues. Client admins and uploaders need a simple way to submit requested files and see status. Partner users need access only to the selected delegated cases they are assigned.

## Product Purpose

TaxoFix BD organizes Bangladesh income tax and VAT service work into one governed workflow: client intake, document upload, file review, return preparation, approval, submission tracking, audit history, and archive. Success means the primary firm can keep sensitive client data controlled, reduce scattered follow-up, see deadlines and workload clearly, and safely assign limited partner help during pressure periods without exposing the full client portfolio.

## Brand Personality

Calm, trustworthy, and operationally sharp. The product should feel premium enough for a professional service firm to show clients, but quiet enough for daily repeated work. It should suggest Bangladesh tax/VAT expertise without looking like a government portal.

## Anti-references

Do not look like a raw admin template, generic fintech landing page, government service portal, consumer banking app, or over-decorated dashboard. Avoid purple-blue gradients, beige monotone palettes, nested card clutter, novelty controls, weak gray text, and language support that only translates a few labels.

## Design Principles

1. Put the working queue first: each screen should make the next tax, VAT, document, or approval action obvious.
2. Preserve firm control: partner and client views must communicate limited, role-aware access rather than a shared open workspace.
3. Earn trust through clarity: use readable hierarchy, explicit statuses, audit-ready labels, and restrained motion.
4. Support bilingual operations fully: English and Bangla should feel equally complete on major surfaces.
5. Keep Bangladesh context practical: use TIN, BIN, VAT periods, Mushak references, challan tracking, and filing deadlines where useful, without turning the interface into an NBR clone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive states. Preserve visible keyboard focus, 44px touch targets on mobile, reduced-motion support, and readable Bangla typography. Avoid relying on color alone for status; pair semantic color with labels and icons.
